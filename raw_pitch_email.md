To get a pre-seed investor’s attention in the African tech ecosystem (like **Pauatua**, **Future Africa**, or **Saviu Ventures**), you need to lead with **traction** and **local insight**. They receive hundreds of "Amazon for Africa" pitches; you need to show them you’ve built "Infrastructure for the Social Seller."

Here is a cold outreach template designed to get a meeting.

---

### **The "Local Infrastructure" Cold Email**

**Subject:** Solving the "Trust Gap" in Social Commerce: Takeer (Pre-Seed)

**Hi [Investor Name],**

I’ve been following **[Venture Firm Name]**’s interest in the **[Tanzanian/East African]** digitizing-trade space.

The biggest bottleneck for the millions of sellers on Instagram and WhatsApp in Tanzania isn't finding customers—it's **trust and logistics**. Buyers are afraid to pay upfront, and merchants struggle with fragmented inter-city bus delivery.

We built **Takeer** to bridge this gap.

Takeer is a frictionless "Link-in-Bio" checkout and escrow layer. We’ve automated the entire trust chain:

* **1-Click Checkout:** Integrated with M-Pesa/Tigo Pesa for instant USSD push.
* **Automated Escrow:** Funds are held until the buyer verifies the item at the bus terminal.
* **Smart Logistics:** We use OCR to track bus waybills and trigger automated last-mile payouts.

We’ve moved past the "idea" stage and have a functional MVP built on a scalable Laravel/Redis architecture. We are currently looking for early-stage partners to help us scale our merchant acquisition across the Mwanza-Dar es Salaam corridor.

Are you open to a 10-minute chat next week to see the demo and our unit economics?

Best,

**[Your Name]**
Founder, Takeer
[Link to your Demo Video or Pitch Deck]

---

### **3 Tips for the "First Meeting"**

1. **Know your "Take-Rate":** If they ask how you scale, remind them of the **5% transaction fee** and the **logistics arbitrage**.
2. **Highlight the "Unboxing Video":** This is your "secret sauce." It shows you understand the fraud culture better than any international competitor.
3. **The "Kariakoo" Factor:** Mention how many merchants in Kariakoo (Dar) and Nyegezi (Mwanza) already use these buses daily. You aren't changing their behavior; you are just making it safer and faster with code.
