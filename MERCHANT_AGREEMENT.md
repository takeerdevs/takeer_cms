2.1 Vendor Warranties: The Vendor explicitly guarantees that all products supplied for live-stream presentation or listed on the social-commerce platform are authentic, legally acquired, and comply with all applicable laws of the United Republic of Tanzania.

## 3. Order Fulfillment and Logistics Liability
3.1 Sole Responsibility: The Vendor retains 100% sole legal and operational responsibility for packaging, shipping, and successfully delivering ordered items to the end consumer.
3.2 Delivery Failure: Any consumer disputes regarding delayed shipments, missing items, or packages damaged in transit must be resolved directly between the Vendor and the shopper.
3.3 Exclusion of Liability: The Company explicitly disclaims all liability for delivery failures. The Company shall not be held liable for any direct or indirect damages arising from the logistics workflow.

4.1 Third-Party Integration: Payment processing is executed via integrated third-party payment aggregators. All transactions are subject to the terms and data privacy policies of the respective payment service providers.

4.3 Refunds and Chargebacks: The Vendor is fully liable for any consumer refunds or payment chargebacks. The Company reserves the right to claw back or deduct refunded amounts from the Vendor’s pending balance.

5.1 Statutory Adherence: Both the Company and the Vendor agree to process all consumer transaction records, phone numbers, and delivery addresses in strict compliance with the Tanzania Personal Data Protection Act (2022) and regulations enforced by the Personal Data Protection Commission (PDPC).
5.2 Data Restrictions: The Vendor is granted consumer data solely for the purpose of fulfilling specific order deliveries. The Vendor is strictly prohibited from selling, sharing, or utilizing consumer databases for unsolicited off-platform marketing without explicit consumer consent.
## 6. Dispute Resolution and Governing Law
6.1 Governing Law: This Agreement shall be governed by, interpreted, and enforced in accordance with the laws of the United Republic of Tanzania.
6.2 Amicable Settlement: In the event of a dispute between the Company and the Vendor arising out of this framework, both parties shall first attempt to reach an amicable settlement through mutual consultation within fourteen (14) days.
6.3 Jurisdiction: Unresolved disputes shall be referred exclusively to the competent courts of law sitting in Dar es Salaam, Tanzania.

### 2. Automated Financial Split (The "Chinese Model" Payout)

To handle the money "like China," you should automate the commission split using a provider like **AzamPay** or **Selcom**:

* **Instant Disbursement**: Use a gateway's "Bulk Disbursement" or "Split Payment" API.
* **The Split**: For a TZS 50,000 sale:
* **70% (TZS 35,000)**: Automatically queued for the merchant.
* **20% (TZS 10,000)**: Your company's operational fee.
* **10% (TZS 5,000)**: Held for the streamer's payout.


* **Timely Payments**: Because you use the API, you can offer your creators "Daily Payouts" once the merchant confirms shipping, which is a massive incentive for young Tanzanians looking for quick income.

### 3. Staying Legally Safe (Tanzania Compliance)

Since you are managing the content and the people, you must follow these specific Tanzanian rules:

* **Withholding Tax**: When paying independent creators, you are legally required to withhold **5% tax** and remit it to the TRA.
* **VFD Receipts**: Your platform must automatically issue the digital TRA receipt via the same DM that contains the checkout link. A provider like **Simplify** offers an API for this for about **TZS 212,400 per year**.

* **Application Fee**: TZS 50,000.

To provide corporate training and gain professional credibility, you will need to register with the **ICT Commission**.

* **ICT Service Provider (Company)**: Registration fees are required, typically totaling around **TZS 100,000 – TZS 200,000**.
